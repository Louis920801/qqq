About Us

Nike was founded on January 25, 1964 by University of Oregon runner Phil Knight and his coach Bill Bowerman. The earliest name was Blue Ribbon Sports. Originally located in Eugene, the business was retailing running shoes for Japan's Onitsuka Tiger (now merged into Asics). Most of the company's sales were made directly from Knight's cars on the side of the runway.

According to University of Oregon student-athlete and 1960 Summer Olympics gold medalist Otis Davis, his coach Bill Bowerman made him his first pair of Nike running shoes. This statement conflicts with saying that the first pair of Nike running shoes were made for Phil Knight. Davis said: "I told Tom Brokaw I was number one. I don't care what the billionaires say. Bill Bowerman made the first pair of shoes for me. No one believed me. I actually don’t like the way they feel on my feet. They don’t provide support and they’re too tight. I’ve looked at Bowerman’s shoes in waffle molds, and they’re made for me.”

In its first yearof operation, Blue Ribbon Sports sold a total of 1,300 pairs of Onitsuka Tiger running shoes, making a profit of $8,000[13]. Sales totaled $20,000 in 1965. Blue Ribbon Sports opened its first retail store in Santa Monica in 1966. In 1967, due to the continuous increase in sales, Blue Ribbon Sports developed to the east coast of the United States and set up a store in Wellesley, Massachusetts.
